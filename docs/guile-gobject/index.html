History

The guile-gobject project was actually one of the very first bindings for the fledgeling GLib type system, back in 2001 when GLib was in its 1.3.x phase. It was developed by Martin Baulig, who pursued the project with obsession. So much so, that he left it with this note:

Today, I mean yesterday - it's already past midnight - was my last day of GNOME 2 hacking for the next four months. Hacking on GNOME was so much fun for me - but now I need to concentrate on my real life and learn for the university.

Hackers beware! Not heeding the warning, Ariel Rios stepped in to steward the project through its teething phases. The GObject wrapper that exists today was primarily written by Martin, however; his successors' jobs have been made much easier because of his high-quality code.

Nothing much happened on the code base until early 2003, when Andy Wingo took an interest in accessing GObject-based APIs with guile. He is the current maintainer, and his principal contribution has been the binding generator, as well as bugfixes. He also maintains the GStreamer bindings, gst-guile.
